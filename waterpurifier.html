The 9-Step Purification Process

Each stage plays a crucial role in transforming contaminated water into pure, safe drinking water

1

Water Inlet

Physics: Gravity and pressure drive contaminated water into the system. Process: Raw water containing visible impurities enters through the inlet valve.

2

Pre-Filter (Mesh Screen)

Physics: Mechanical filtration using mesh barriers. Process: Large particles like sand, rust, and debris (>100 microns) are trapped by the screen filter.

3

Sediment Filtration

Physics: Multi-layer depth filtration through porous media. Process: Water passes through polypropylene, cotton, and sand layers removing particles down to 5 microns.

4

Activated Carbon Filter

Physics: Adsorption - contaminants stick to carbon's porous surface (surface area: 500-1500 m²/g). Process: Chlorine, pesticides, VOCs, and odors are chemically absorbed into carbon granules.

5

RO Membrane (Reverse Osmosis)

Physics: Semi-permeable membrane (0.0001 micron pores) + pressure (40-100 PSI) forces water molecules through while blocking larger contaminants. Process: 95-99% of dissolved salts, heavy metals, bacteria, and viruses are rejected. Pure water passes through; waste water is flushed out.

6

UV Purification

Physics: UV-C light (254 nm wavelength) damages microbial DNA, preventing reproduction. Process: Water flows through a UV chamber where 99.99% of bacteria, viruses, and protozoa are neutralized without chemicals.

7

UF / Post-Carbon Polishing

Physics: Ultrafiltration (0.01 micron) + final carbon contact. Process: Final removal of any remaining particles and taste enhancement. Water becomes crystal clear with optimal taste.

8

Storage Tank

Physics: Pressurized storage with air bladder maintains flow. Process: Purified water is stored in a food-grade tank with anti-bacterial coating. Digital sensors monitor purity levels (TDS: 10-50 ppm).

9

Clean Water Output

Physics: Controlled flow through dispensing valve. Process: 100% purified, safe drinking water flows through the tap. Quality: 99.9% contaminant-free, pH balanced (6.5-8.5).

Frequently Asked Questions

Learn more about water purification technology

What is RO (Reverse Osmosis)? ▼

Reverse Osmosis is a water purification process that uses a semi-permeable membrane to remove ions, molecules, and larger particles. By applying pressure, water is forced through the membrane while contaminants are left behind. RO can remove up to 99% of dissolved salts, bacteria, viruses, and heavy metals, making it one of the most effective purification methods.

What does UV purification do? ▼

UV (Ultraviolet) purification uses UV-C light at 254 nanometers wavelength to destroy the DNA of microorganisms like bacteria, viruses, and protozoa. This prevents them from reproducing and makes them harmless. UV treatment is chemical-free, doesn't alter water taste, and is effective against 99.99% of waterborne pathogens.

Why is carbon filtration important? ▼

Activated carbon has an incredibly porous structure with a massive surface area (500-1500 m²/g). Through adsorption, it attracts and traps chlorine, volatile organic compounds (VOCs), pesticides, and chemicals that cause bad taste and odor. Carbon filtration significantly improves water taste and removes harmful organic contaminants that other filters might miss.

How often should filters be replaced? ▼

Filter replacement depends on usage and water quality. Generally: Pre-filters (3-6 months), Carbon filters (6-12 months), RO membrane (2-3 years), UV lamp (12 months). Regular maintenance ensures optimal performance and water quality. Most modern systems have indicator lights to alert you when replacement is needed.

What is TDS and why does it matter? ▼

TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) measures the concentration of dissolved substances in water, measured in parts per million (ppm). High TDS can indicate contamination. Ideal drinking water TDS is 50-150 ppm. RO systems typically reduce TDS to 10-50 ppm. While some minerals are beneficial, excessive TDS can include harmful contaminants like heavy metals and salts.
